Look to the north: blocking the northward passage of the rivers which form this lake, a great continental ice sheet once towered above the horizon as far as the eye can see.

When the glacier melted, it left a wide moraine - a plain of rocks and sand - that extended to Rathdrum and formed this lake. This is as far south as any continental ice sheet ever reached into Idaho. Lake Facts: Area - 50 square miles: Length - 32 miles: Elevation - 2,124 feet.

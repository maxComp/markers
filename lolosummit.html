The Lewis and Clark party crossed this pass September 13, 1805, westbound for the Pacific after a long detour from the south. From the headwaters of the Missouri, they had crossed the mountains to the Salmon.

Finding that river impassable, they traded for packhorses, hired an Indian guide and came north to an Indian trail across the mountains here. Tired and ill-fed, the men had a hard struggle in early snow along the steep ridges which the trail followed for most of its 125-mile course west to the Clearwater River.

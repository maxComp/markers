In order to cross Lawyer's Canyon and other Camas Prairie gorges, a series of high railroad bridges was built in 1908.

This highway goes past two of them here. Most were timber, but a metal structure, 1,500 feet long and 296 feet high, was needed here. A long, high wooden trestle also crossed a nearby side canyon. North Idaho's Camas Prairie Railroad - a joint Northern Pacific and Union Pacific venture - served this region for eight decades.

On October 28, 1983, a major earthquake fracture, 26 miles long and seven miles deep, surfaced as the Lost River Valley slid away from Mount Borah.

During that rock shift, Mount Borah's ridge front rose about six inches, while this valley subsided nine feet. This kind of movement has been going on here for 10 to 20 million years as subsurface rock has been pulled apart during gradual but persistent range and valley building. You can drive to a spectacular fracture that shattered this side road 2.5 miles from here.

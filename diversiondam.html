Diversion Dam was completed in 1909 to lift water into an already constructed New York Canal system, greatly expanding its irrigated farmlands.

After a quarter-century of failing to dig a large canal above Diversion Dam, United States Reclamation Service funding enabled a group of Boise Valley irrigation districts to complete this project. Then in 1912, a generating plant was installed to provide power to construct Arrowrock Dam. It has been preserved as a historical display by the Bureau of Reclamation.

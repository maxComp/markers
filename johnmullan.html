John Mullan was the Army officer who in 1859-62 surveyed and built the Mullan Road from Walla Walla, Washington, to Fort Benton, Montana.

The road was to connect the Missouri and the Columbia rivers. Congress approved it in 1855. Indian troubles and lack of funds delayed the job, but the road was completed in 1862. The first route in 1859 had passed about six miles west of here, but floods forced a change, and the final road passed north of Coeur d'Alene Lake. Interstate 90 follows Mullan's final route.

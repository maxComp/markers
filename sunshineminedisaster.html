On May 2, 1972, fire broke out in the mine.

Carbon monoxide, heat, smoke and gas spread quickly through the tunnels, severely hampering rescue efforts. One hundred seventy-six miners were working at various levels. Eighty-five made it out to safety. Seven days later, two miners were found alive. On May 13, the last of the 91 victims were brought out. This was the country's worst hardrock mine disaster since 1917. The Sunshine Silver Mine is a mile deep and has 100 miles of tunnels.

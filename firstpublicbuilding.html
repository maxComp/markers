In 1870, Brigham Young appointed Ezra T. Clark, David Hess and Nicholas Barkdull to colonize this area.

Originally known as Twin Creeks, Joseph C. Rich surveyed the site in 1871. In 1872, the new settlement was named Georgetown in honor of George O. Cannon. The first public building used as a church, school and community center is situated 1 block south and 1/2 block west of here. In 1966 the building was given to the Daughters of Utah Pioneers and is now a pioneer Relic Hall.

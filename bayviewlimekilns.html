Large kilns that produced lime from 1904 to 1932 still can be visited in Bayview, about two miles from here.

Production of lime for nearby mines and Spokane buildings commenced in this area as soon as rail transportation and markets became available. For 14 years after 1887, steamboats hauled lime to a Northern Pacific Railway dock at Hope. When an interurban rail connection reached here in 1911, lime for cement products was exported from Bayview until an economic depression destroyed regional demand for concrete.

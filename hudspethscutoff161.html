Here, at a landmark called Sheep Rock, Hudspeth's Cutoff left the Oregon Trail and struck straight west to California.

Stampeding 49'ers would try anything to save miles and time in their rush for California gold. Earlier emigrant wagons headed northwest to Fort Hall, but on July 19, 1849, Benoni Hudspeth and John Myers led their party west along an old Indian trail they had checked out across rough country in 1848. Their difficult cutoff immediately became popular, though it saved only about 25 miles and two days' travel.

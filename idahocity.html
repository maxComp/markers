This roaring metropolis was founded early in October 1862, about 10 weeks after gold was discovered in Boise Basin.

By the next summer, this was the largest city in the Northwest, with 6,275 people - 5,691 of them men! Families followed, and respectable businesses, schools, libraries, good theaters, churches and fraternal orders soon came. The town survived several diasterous fires and remained an important mining center until the war shut down gold production in 1942.

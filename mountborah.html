Idaho's highest peak, 12,662 feet, is named for William E. Borah, who served in the United States Senate from 1907 until his death in 1940.

Ten or a dozen large but shallow inland seas have covered this area in the past billion years. They became a graveyard for countless generations of sea creatures: bones, shells, coral and microscopic remains piled up through the eons into a clay-and-limestone deposit thousands of feet thick. During the past 10 or 20 million years, part of this deposit has been thrust upward into the towering ridge you see before you.

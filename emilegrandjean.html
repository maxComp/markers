An emigrant from Denmark, where he had studied forestry; Grandjean came to this part of Idaho in 1883 to mine, hunt and trap.

Before Idaho became a state in 1890, he built a winter cabin below Grandjean Peak at a site later occupied by the Grandjean ranger station. Because of his European studies, he became a professional forester here. He served as supervisor of the Boise National Forest from 1906-22.

Long before white men discovered these springs, September 9, 1812, Indians gathered here to use the free hot water.

Except where they found hot springs, prehistoric Indians had a hard time getting hot water. They wove water-tight baskets into which they put heated rocks. Here they had plenty of hot water for baths and for processing hides without going to all the work of heating baskets. This was one of their major campgrounds, especially in the winter. After 1868, when they began to stay mostly on the Fort Hall Indian Reservation, this spot lost its importance as a winter camp.

A bad ford gave trouble to wagon trains crossing this stream on the trail to California and Oregon in 1849.

In that year, gold-seeking 49'rs developed a shortcut that crossed here. Then emigrants built two bridges here in 1850. But an enterprising toll collector came along and charged $1 per wagon, which was more than some could afford. Penniless emigrants, who had to make an eightmile detour, cursed, while their richer companions comfortably clattered across both bridges.

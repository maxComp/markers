Archaeological excavations show human occupation of the Snake River plain for more than 10,000 years.

Early men left weapons and other gear in a cave in a nearby butte. Bones show that they hunted game that is now extinct - camels, ancient horses and ground sloths. In succeeding thousands of years the climate grew extremely dry, much drier than it is today. Still later, it became less arid again. Through all these changes, man succeeded in adapting and remained here.

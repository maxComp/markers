Since 1949, more nuclear reactors - more than 50 of them - have been built on this plain than anywhere else in the world.

This 900-square-mile Idaho National Engineering and Environmental Laboratory is the birthplace of the nuclear Navy. Commercial power reactor prototypes, including reactors that breed more fuel than they consume, were developed here. Also, internationally renowned for its materials testing reactors and reactor programs, this laboratory has become a major research center for developing peaceful uses of atomic energy.

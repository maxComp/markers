Named for George Grimes, who with Moses Splawn led the party that on August 2, 1862, made the strike that started the Boise Basin gold rush.

The party was searching for a rich basin described to Splawn a year earlier by an Indian. Further up this creek, they found the gold they were looking for. A few days later, Grimes was killed at Grimes Pass (it was blamed on Indians), and his partners had to bury him in a prospect hole nearby.

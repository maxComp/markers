Founded as a mining town in 1884, Wallace became a railroad center in 1887 and Shoshone County seat in 1889.

Rebuilt after a disasterous fire in 1890, Wallace has preserved its pioneer mining heritage. North Idaho's two million-acre forest fire was stopped here in 1910, and business buildings of that era survive in an impressive historic district. A museum in Wallace's restored 1901 Northern Pacific Depot, on Sixth Street, interprets the region's railway history

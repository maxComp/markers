Expanding from a two-year community college (1932-1965) to a campus with a graduate program, Boise State was designated as a university in 1974.

Originating as an Episcopalian academy founded in 1892, this institution was located a mile north until 1940, when Boise's municipal airport, located here, became available for a large new campus. Christ's Chapel, Boise's original Episcopalian church building which was built downtown in 1886, was moved to this site for permanent preservation in 1963.

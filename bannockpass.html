This traditional Indian route provided access from Montana's buffalo country to Snake and Salmon river fishing streams.

Hudson's Bay Co. trapping expeditions came this way after 1822, and prospectors followed, searching for mines. Then in 1877, Nez Perce war combatants returned from Montana to Idaho through Bannock Pass on their way from Big Hole battleground to Yellowstone Park and Canada. From 1910 to 1939, railroad trains from Dillon to Salmon steamed through here. Old Gilmore and Pittsburgh railroad tunnels and grades still can be seen from this highway.

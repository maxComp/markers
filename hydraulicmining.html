Visible directly across the river is a pit left by large-scale hydraulic mining for gold deposited in ancient gravel beds.

Big nozzles, called "hydraulic giants," shot powerful streams of water against a prehistoric river bed (now the bank of the stream) to expose and wash down goldbearing gravel, which then in the 1860s, remained in use until recent economic changes made gold mining unprofitable. Many pits - some enormously larger than this one - are left as reminders of the mining days gone by.

A vast mountain wilderness, cut by the mile deep Salmon River Canyon, stretches across Idaho south and east of here.

Travel through the Salmon River Mountains always was hard in the early days. An 1872 railway survey showed the Salmon River Canyon to be too expensive a route to build. Until a highway was finished down White Bird Hill in 1921, only some pack trails and a difficult wagon road crossed the rugged mountain barrier that separated north and south Idaho.

John Grey discovered this valley in 1818 or 1819 while hunting beaver for Donald Mackenzie's North West Co. trappers.

An Iroquois leader, also known as Ignace Hatchioraquasha, he also explored Grey's River nearby in Wyoming. Aside from his trapping skills, he was noted for his unusual aptitude in fighting grizzly bears. After trapping in this country for 20 years, he retired with his Iroquois band in 1836 to help found Kansas City, Missouri.

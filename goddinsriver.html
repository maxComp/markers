Known as Goddin's River in the days of the fur trade, this stream originally was named for the fur trapper who discovered it.

Thyery Goddin, a prominent Iroquois who explored this river in 1819 or 1820, had come here with Donald Mackenzie's fur hunters who worked for the North West Company of Montreal. Well stocked with beaver until it was trapped out in 1824, Goddin's River offered a wealth of furs to early trappers. Then, after the fur trade was over, the river's original name was forgotten. Later settlers called it the Lost River because it sinks into the desert lava.

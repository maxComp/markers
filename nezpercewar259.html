After the Clearwater battle on the heights above here, July 11-12, 1877, the Indians crossed the Lolo Trail to Montana.

Advancing northward along the high ground with 600 troops and artillery, General 0.0. Howard found the Indian camp on the flat across the river. But 24 brave Indians blocked his advance and 100 more pinned him in rifle pits for a day. Then the Indians, camp and all, moved slowly northward past Kamiah, while Howard followed without fighting. There the Indians decided to move east away from the troops.

John Welch, always known as Packer John, hauled supplies from Lewiston to Idaho City during a major Boise Basin gold rush of 1863-64.

He built a cabin (one-quarter mile north of here) that immediately became an Idaho landmark. Territorial political conventions (Republican in 1863 and Democratic in 1864) used his cabin as a point where north Idaho leaders could meet with southern representatives to choose congressional candidates. This site was a state park and is now an Adams County park.

Rising as a small stream in the valley to the south, the Salmon River winds 420 miles across Idaho before flowing into the Snake River.

Discovered in 1805 by Meriwether Lewis and William Clark, and explored with great difficulty by fur traders and prospectors, the Salmon River drains a vast tangle of rugged mountains and deep canyons. Until 1950, boats could not ascend the main canyon, so the Salmon got the name "River of No Return." Large tracts of untamed wilderness still are found in the Salmon River Mountains.

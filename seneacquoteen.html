Long before white men discovered this river, Indians used to camp here at this important early crossing.

Fur traders, surveyors and miners followed the old Indian trail that forded the river here at Seneacquoteen - a Kalispell word meaning "crossing." During the Kootenay gold rush of 1864, a wagon road came from Walla Walla to a ferry here. The Wild Horse Trail - a pack route - ran on north to the Kootenay mines in British Columbia.

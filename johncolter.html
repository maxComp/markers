John Colter discovered this valley in 1808 while exploring the Yellowstone and upper Snake country in search of beaver.

Setting out by himself with his gun and a 30-pound pack, he tried to get the Indians to join in his trapping business. On the way here from a Yellowstone post 240 miles to the northeast, he came upon Colter's Hell - some hot springs near Cody, Wyoming. On his way back, he explored Yellowstone Park. In the spring of 1810, after several perilous escapes from the Blackfeet, he returned to Missouri, lucky to get back alive.

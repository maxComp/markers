"A bluff jolly good fellow," he joined the Rocky Mountain fur trade in 1829, married a Nez Perce in 1838 and settled with the Lapwai band in 1840.

In 1850, the Oregon Donation Land Act gave free farms to pioneers who had come to the Oregon Territory. Craig had the only eligible farm in Idaho (then part of Oregon) and filed a 640-acre claim here. When the Nez Perce reservation was set up, the Indians trusted Craig and let him keep his farm.

Once gold miners passed through the area in the 1860s, it wasn't long before several bold entrepreneurs settled in the fertile land along the Payette River and established a small community that eventually became known as the town of Emmett.

With plenty of water available from the Payette River, some of these early settlers planted gardens and fruit trees and helped supply food to the mining camps in the mountains. By the time an elaborate canal system had been developed, and with the completion of Black Canyon Dam above the town in 1924, hundreds of orchards had been planted in what became known as the Valley of Plenty.

Apples, peaches, apricots, pears, prunes and plums were available for market, and by 1940 Emmett had become the largest shipping point in Idaho for sweet cherries. With abundant harvests of all that fruit, it stands to reason that early residents would choose to celebrate their good fortune, and so in 1928 the tradition of the Cherry Festival was born.

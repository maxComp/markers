South-central Idaho's rail center since 1882 when trains reached here, Shoshone has a historic district of unusual interest.

Branch rail lines to the Wood River and Camas Prairie served distant farmers and miners, while a stage line to Shoshone Falls accommodated wealthy tourists who visited Idaho's foremost 19th-century attraction. Vast sheep grazing lands made this a major early center for Basque herders. Use of lava rock for building construction gives Shoshone a distinctive historic character.

In 1874, Bishop L.H. Hatch built a mansion that has been preserved as a fine example of pioneer Idaho architecture.

Idaho's only railroad, serving Montana's thriving mining camps, reached here that year during a time of depression between gold rushes. At the time, Franklin was Idaho's largest city. Two years later, rail construction resumed, and freighters moved on. But Hatch's elegant house remains as a reminder of a bygone era.

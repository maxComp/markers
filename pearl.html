Long after its discovery in 1867, a mining camp flourished next to a gold lode below Crown Point on this ridge 4 miles west of here.

Pearl boasted 3 stores, 4 saloons, a butcher shop, a fire station, a church, a school, 2 hotels, and an Odd Fellows' Lodge before it declined. A revival was considered as late as 1882, when high gold prices had increased Pearl's early production value to $12,000,000.

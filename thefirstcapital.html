The organization of Idaho Territory was proclaimed in Lewiston July 10, 1863, and the first two legislatures met here.

When Lewiston served as the capital, Idaho Territory included Montana and practically all of Wyoming - an area much larger than Texas. Then in 1864, after Montana was established as a separate territory and most of Wyoming was attached to Dakota, the governor and legislature decided to locate the capital of Idaho in Boise, where it has been ever since.

At the junction of the Rocky Bar road with the Oregon Trail, this was a major stage line stop for 20 years.

Stage service commenced in 1864, and a connection to the Rocky Bar mines was moved here in 1870. In 1878, the station owners thought it would sound a lot better to call their place Mountain Home instead of Rattlesnake. Then the Union Pacific - built out in the valley in 1883 - replaced the freight wagons and stage lines that came through here. So Mountain Home was moved on down Rattlesnake Creek to its present location on the railroad.

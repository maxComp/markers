Farmhouses and pioneer buildings of an old Mormon community have been preserved at Chesterfield, 16 miles north of here.

Located in 1879 by a Mormon bishop, Chesterfield offered a good opportunity to farmers from Utah, which then was becoming overcrowded. Within two years, a group of settlers helped construct a nearby railroad that made their town grow to more than 400 people. Chesterfield thrived for more than a generation before new trends in farming and transportation led to its decline.

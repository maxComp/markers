You have just crossed a small canyon that Oregon Trail emigrants regarded as their most dangerous exposure to Indian hostility.

After 1854, they had good reason to be alarmed. Wagon traffic had ruined important traditional Indian trails. Thousands of oxen, horses, sheep and cattle had overgrazed a broad zone along their trail, leading to Indian resentment. Worse yet, a few emigrants had shot enough Indians to provoke a great deal of bitterness. On August 9, 1862, Pocatello's Shoshoni band resisted further wagon traffic here, trapping a small emigrant party in a deep gully An unusually fine stretch of wagon tracks leading to that site can be reached by a marked trail from here.

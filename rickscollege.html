Ricks College commenced as a Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints stake academy in 1888.

Ricks was first known as Bannock Stake Academy, then Remont Stake Academy, Smith Academy and Ricks Academy before becoming Ricks College in 1918. The name memorializes Thomas E. Ricks, founder of Rexburg in 1883. Today Ricks College, Idaho's largest private college, includes a modern campus surrounding the historic 1903 Jacob Spori building.

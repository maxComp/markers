After prospecting north of here from 1878 to 1882, A.J. Prichard showed a few fortune hunters where to find gold.

More than a year later, a horde of miners rushed there to start Eagle City. A permanent camp followed at Murray, January 22, 1884. As Shoshone County seat (1885-98) and this area's gold center, Murray flourished for more than a decade. Important buildings - a courthouse, Masonic Hall and other landmarks - have been preserved there to reflect life in a gold rush camp a century ago.

The name applied to these mountains and the whole surrounding region is an outdated spelling of the word "Hawaii."

Fur-trading ships brought Hawaiian natives - then called "Owyhees" - to the Northwest. In 1818, Donald Mackenzie brought the first big brigade of fur hunters to the Snake River Valley. He sent several Owyhees to trap in this region and they never came back. Ever since then, this has been called the Owyhee country and Owyhee County carries on the name. (Pronounce "Owyhee" and "Hawaii" aloud - they sound similar.)

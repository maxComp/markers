Archaeological research has traced human occupation of this valley back more than 10,000 years.

The first men here found the valley forested. As the climate became drier, other mountain dwellers - known to archaeologists as people of the Bitterroot culture - settled here, perhaps about 8,000 years ago. These forerunners of the modern northern Shoshoni Indians lived in family bands and hunted big game, such as bison and mountain sheep.

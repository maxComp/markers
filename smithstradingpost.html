In 1848, Pegleg Smith started a trading post on the Oregon Trail at Big Timber on the Bear River about a mile northwest of here.

Some travelers called it Fort Smith, though it had only four log cabins and some Indian lodges. Packing a plow and tools from Salt Lake City, Smith (a mountain man who had to amputate his own leg 20 years before) tried unsuccessfully to raise crops. But he did a big business when the California gold rush of 1849 brought thousands past here. The 49'rs reported that he had many horses and cattle and was making $100 a day.

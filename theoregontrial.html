The Oregon Trail is still clearly visible coming off the rimrock across the river.

Here the westbound emigrants after 1840 came gratefully down into this green valley: The first cart passed here with Henry H. Spalding and Marcus Whitman, pioneer missionaries, in 1836. After 1842, thousands of emigrant wagons cut a broad track, later called the Overland Road. The tide of travel declined when the railroad was completed in 1883, but emigrant wagons continued to use this road until after 1900. The tracks of the wagons and stages can still be followed for miles east in the desert.

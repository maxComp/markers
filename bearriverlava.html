Until about 28,000 years ago, the Bear River used to flow northwest from here through the Portneuf Canyon into the Snake River.

Lava eruptions blocked that route, diverting the Bear River south into what is now Salt Lake. At that time, a large inland sea - known as Lake Bonneville - covered much of Utah. Additional water from the Bear River helped it overflow into the Snake River before a change in climate dried it up about 8,000 years ago.

This important archaeological site was occupied for 10,000 years or more and has at least 10 pit houses as much as 5,000 years old.

Two styles of houses were used. Some were fairly square with interior benches dug out for use by a family or two. Others were round - 20 to 30 feet wide and two to three feet deep - but lacked benches. This village reached its height from about 4,100 to 2,600 years ago, but remained important enough that when fur traders arrived in 1812, they made this their main camp.

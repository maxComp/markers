Before settlers came to Idaho in 1860, buffalo used to roam through this valley.

Most of them had left here by 1840. After they acquired Spanish horses, 18th-century Shoshoni buffalo hunters could drive a small herd over a cliff to make their work easier. Directly north of here, an old buffalo jump, used before mining commenced in this region, can be clearly seen. Archaeologists also have found stone weapon factories near it.
